# 👑 Student #01 Custom 4-Week Curriculum (The VIP Plan)

> **Student Profile**: 20yo, British (Black), TikTok model/influencer with 1M+ followers
> **Goals**: High-priority survival Korean (Taxis, Cafes) + Immediate street reactions for fan interactions
> **Strict Rules**: No spelling corrections, no vowel pronunciation drills, absolutely NO long/complex grammar explanations.

---

## 🎯 Core Strategy (The Approach)

1. **Role-Play Driven Practical Training**
   - 80% of the lesson will be role-playing. The instructor will act as the "talkative taxi driver," "super fan," or "cafe barista."
2. **Personalized "Cheat Sheet" on Google Docs**
   - Instead of thick textbooks, we will create a dedicated Google Doc containing simple, 1-liner reaction phrases that she can pull up on her smartphone anytime.
3. **Equipping "Trendy & Cute" Nuances**
   - Teach trendy and natural-sounding Korean suitable for a 20yo model/influencer. (e.g., instead of the robotic, textbook "감사합니다(Thank you)", teach the natural "우와 진짜요? 너무 고마워요~ (Wow really? Thanks so much~)").

## 📅 4-Week Curriculum (Once a week, 1 hour/session)

### 🚕 [Week 1] Survival Stage 1: Handling Talkative Taxi Drivers
*Crushing her most urgent need in the very first week to guarantee 100% satisfaction.*

*   **Scenario 1: Stating and changing destinations**
    *   Super simple survival phrases: "기사님, OO(으)로 가주세요 (Sir, please go to OO)", "여기서 세워주세요 (Please pull over here)."
*   **Scenario 2: [Core] Politely cutting off conversations (Cultural Coaching)**
    *   Explain the Korean "Elder-Youth" culture (why older drivers talk so much, and how to disengage without offending them).
    *   **Magic Defense Phrases**: Intense training on reactions to use when she wants to escape a conversation with a smile.
        *   "아 네~ (Ah, yes~ *smiles*)", "잘 모르겠어요~ (I'm not too sure~)", "제가 한국말을 아직 잘 못해서요~ 죄송해요 😅 (I still don't speak Korean very well~ Sorry 😅)"
    *   **Role Play**: Instructor fully immerses into the role of a nosy taxi driver.

### 💞 [Week 2] Business Stage 1: Street Fan Service (Fan Interactions)
*A highly customized lesson targeting her identity as a TikToker/Model.*

*   **Characteristics of Korean Fans (Cultural Coaching)**: 
    *   Why fans hesitate to approach, and the specific nuances Koreans use when asking for photos.
*   **Immediate Reactions for Fan Encounters (The 1-Second Rule)**
    *   When touched/moved: "헐! 대박! (Omg! Wow!)", "진짜요? 너무 감사해요 ㅠㅠ (Really? Thank you so much!)"
    *   When complimented (e.g., "You're so pretty"): "아니에요~ (No way~ *waves hand*)", "완전 부끄러워요ㅎㅎ (I'm so shy/flattered haha)"
*   **Manners when taking a photo together**
    *   "같이 찍어요! (Let's take it together!)", "하나 둘 셋~ 📸 (One, two, three~)" 
    *   *Drill these sentences until they become muscle memory.*

### ☕ [Week 3] Survival Stage 2: Navigating Hotspots (Cafes & Shopping)
*How not to panic when buying necessities or going to a cafe alone.*

*   **Scenario 1: Mastering Cafe Orders**
    *   Completely decode the 3 inevitable questions Korean baristas ask: (For here or to go? Do you need a receipt? Point accumulation?)
    *   "아이스 아메리카노 하나 포장이요. (One iced Americano to go, please.)" (Ordering with zero unnecessary words).
*   **Scenario 2: The Olive Young / Supermarket Defense (Cultural Coaching)**
    *   How not to panic when a store clerk approaches to kindly explain products.
    *   "괜찮아요, 저 혼자 볼게요~ 😊 (It's okay, I'll just look around on my own~ *smiles*)" 

### 📱 [Week 4] Business Stage 2: First Vlog/Streaming Opening in Korean
*Acting as her cheerleader to help her reach her ultimate goal: expanding into Vlogs/Streaming.*

*   **Crafting Opening/Closing Remarks**
    *   Instead of a generic "Hello, my name is...", co-create a trendy opening phrase that her fans will go crazy for. (e.g., "안녕 예쁜이들~ (Hi cuties~)")
*   **1-Month Wrap-up & Next Steps**
    *   Review the Google Doc cheat sheet together on the phone.
    *   Naturally drive retention for the next month: "Next month, let's practice more advanced interactions for your streaming!"

## 📌 Special Guidelines for the Founder (Instructor)

1. **Strictly NO Korean Spelling Correction**: It's totally fine if she can't distinguish ㅖ from ㅔ. If she draws letters like pictures, just say "Wow, cute!" and move on. She is not training to be a news anchor.
2. **Write pronunciations in English on the Google Doc**: When she's in the field, trying to read Hangul on the spot will cause panic. Always provide honest, easy-to-read English pronunciations next to the sentences. (e.g., 저 혼자 볼게요 -> Jeo hon-ja bol-ge-yo)
3. **Subtly request photos/videos for reviews**: Since you live in the same building, it's easier to approach casually. Say something like, "Our first class together is such a special memory for me too! Should we take a picture for Insta?" Naturally induce a shoutout/verification. This will become a massive portfolio piece later.
