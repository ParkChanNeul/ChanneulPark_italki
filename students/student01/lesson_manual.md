# 📖 The "Korean Connection" Lesson Manual

**Welcome to our journey!** 
Before our first class this Sunday, I want to share my core philosophy on how we will work together. My goal is to make every minute of our 1-hour session 100% focused on real-world survival and making you sound completely natural.

Here is how we will maximize your learning:

---

## 1. I am your "Mentor", not a "Teacher"
**Language is NOT studying. It is a habit and practice.** If you learn something but never use it, you will lose it. 
In our sessions, I will not teach you long grammar rules from a textbook. Instead, we will focus on two things:
1. **Cultural Translation**: Instead of analyzing grammar, I will help you understand **WHY Koreans use certain expressions and the cultural nuances behind them**.
2. **Your Personal 'Cheat Sheet'**: Instead of heavy textbooks, I will create a live Google Doc for you. It will contain quick, 1-line survival reactions you can check on your phone whenever you panic on the street.

## 2. The "200 Words" Goal & Light Missions 📱
For your survival and career growth in Korea, there are roughly **200 essential vocabulary words** that are great to know. 

*   **App-Based Vocabulary**: Please continue using Busuu, Teuida, or any app you prefer. Your "homework" will simply be to keep building your vocabulary through the app. 
*   **The "Field Mission"**: Instead of boring textbook assignments, I’ll give you fun, real-world challenges. (e.g., "Try using this exact sentence at the convenience store this week and let’s talk about how it went!")
*   **My Role**: You build the basic "Korean muscles" via apps on your own time. When we meet, we will use those words in fun, unpredictable conversations so you get used to speaking naturally.

## 3. Bonus Experience: "The Field Trip" ☕🛍️
Since we live in the same building, we have an amazing advantage! Our official class is 1 hour indoors, but...
Once you feel comfortable, **we can actually go downstairs to a real restaurant or cafe together during my free time after class.** 
Here is our deal: You buy the coffee or meal, and in exchange, I will be right there behind you to mentor and help you actually order and interact with real Korean staff! Having a local Korean friend backing you up will give you massive confidence. It’s the perfect real-world practice in exchange for a cup of coffee.

## 4. Preparation for Sunday 📝
*   **No heavy textbooks needed.** Just bring your smartphone and yourself.
*   **Think of a "Failed Moment"**: If you have any recent moments where you felt frustrated or panicked because you couldn't speak in Korean (like in a taxi or a store), bring that story! We will solve it.
*   **Google Docs**: I will create a live "Cheat Sheet" for you during our class. You'll be able to check it on your phone anytime you walk around Seoul.

See you this Sunday! 🔥

## 5. Visual Grammar System ← NEW (첫 수업 피드백 반영)
Our lessons use a **color-coded visual grammar** system. When I write Korean sentences for you, I'll use colors to help you instantly see the structure:

- 🔵 **Blue** = Grammar point (today's focus, verb endings, particles)
- 🟢 **Green** = Vocabulary & meaning units (nouns, key words)
- 🟡 **Yellow** = Cultural points (things that work differently from English)

**How it works:**
- During class: I'll write on my iPad in color → you write it back
- After class: Everything gets organized into your Google Doc
- On the street: Pull up Google Doc on your phone → instant cheat sheet

**Example:**
> 🟢저는 🟢커피를 🔵마셔요
> (I) (coffee) (drink - polite present ← today's grammar!)

## 6. The Four Rules of Our Lessons ← NEW
These four rules guide every single class we do:

1. **SHOW IT** — I'll always write it down visually, not just say it
2. **WAIT** — When I ask you a question, take your time! I will never rush you
3. **BRIDGE** — I'll always tell you when we're switching topics ("Great job with X! Now let's try Y")
4. **SPLIT** — Korean for practice, English for explanations. You'll always understand WHY
